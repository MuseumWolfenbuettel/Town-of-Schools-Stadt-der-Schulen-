15.Town of Schools – Stadt der Schulen

For many years Wolfenbüttel bore the byname “town of schools and gardens”. Towards the close of the 19th century there were three exceptional schools in Wolfenbüttel that were known far beyond the duchy's borders. The girls' school in the castle and the Neu-Watzum boarding school for girls represent a departure in female higher education, which ultimately led to government-certified public education and training for women. The Samson School was a Jewish free school of trans-regional significance, which, after 1881, became interdenominational.
